Taxonomy refinement

Passive Team-member as a pre-refinement boundary case

Some experts questioned whether Passive Team-member agents should be considered team members at all. We incorporated this feedback by removing Passive Team-member agents from the final taxonomy. Although passive roles exist in human organizations, the goal of the taxonomy is not to mimic all human organizational patterns, but to define meaningful and desirable agent roles for engineered SE workflows.

Why this category may still appear in validation plots. Passive Team-member agents were included in the initial expert elicitation but later removed after considering expert feedback. Therefore, validation figures may still report this category as a pre-refinement boundary case for traceability.

Interpretation

How to read the validation

The validation results should be interpreted as qualitative plausibility and relevance signals. They support the usefulness of the taxonomy and identify boundary cases, especially for team-member and managerial agents. They do not constitute statistical evidence of adoption, impact, or industry prevalence.
