Open questions and research agenda

This page reports the open questions triggered by the taxonomy and turns them into testable empirical questions. It is an outcome-oriented page: it suggests follow-up studies rather than serving as evidence for the taxonomy.

Questions

Triggered open questions

QuestionTestable research questionSuggested methods
Q1
Tools or team members?		To what extent do software engineers perceive different categories of Agentic AI as tools versus team members, and which factors influence this perception?Field studies in industrial teams; controlled human-AI pair programming experiments; longitudinal surveys.
Q2
Peers or leaders?		Under which technical and organizational conditions are Agentic AI systems accepted as peers or leaders in SE teams?Controlled coordination/planning experiments; organizational simulations; interviews with developers and team leaders.
Q3
Empathy and soft skills?		What is the impact of empathic or socially aware behaviors exhibited by Agentic AI on communication quality, developer satisfaction, and team performance?A/B experiments; laboratory collaborative tasks; conversation analysis.
Q4
Trust?		How does developer trust in Agentic AI vary with the agent's level of autonomy, transparency, and explainability?Longitudinal studies; controlled experiments manipulating explainability; validated trust-in-automation questionnaires.
Q5
Education and workforce?		How does adoption of Agentic AI with increasing autonomy affect learning outcomes, skill development, and professional identity?Longitudinal classroom studies; controlled educational experiments; curriculum and competency analysis.
Q6
Social presence?		Do soft skills remain critical for effective SE teamwork in environments augmented by advanced Agentic AI?Mixed-method field studies; group performance analysis; ethnographic observations.
Q7
Bounded AI at work?		What governance models and regulatory mechanisms do software engineers consider acceptable for constraining or bounding Agentic AI in the workplace?Large-scale developer surveys; cross-cultural studies; policy and labor-agreement analysis; participatory focus groups.
